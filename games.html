games


Daniel is a student at Boise State University seeking degrees in GIMM (Gaming, Interactive Media, Mobile technology), and Music Performance. Daniel enjoys all aspects of game development, but most of his experience is from 3D modeling, programming, and music composition. Daniel uses many different applications to work on creating games/assets; some of these include Blender, Maya, Unity, Unreal Engine 4, Logic Pro X, and Finale. While Daniel is only in his second year of the GIMM program, he has already had some great achievements. In 2016, he and his team won 1st place and best design at the BSU Appathon. He also competed with a team at the 2016 IVRC Immerse-athon VR/AR competition, and won best student team. Daniel currently works at the GIMMworks lab at Boise State, giving him great opportunities for experiencing real-world projects as a student.
