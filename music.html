music



Daniel Quatrone is a student at Boise State University pursuing a double major in Euphonium Performance and GIMM (Gaming, Interactive Media, Mobile technology). Daniel started playing Trombone in 9th grade, and Euphonium a year after, in which he won 1st place in the Idaho State Solo Competition on Euphonium. He also performed with the Boise Youth Philharmonic Orchestra. In 11th grade, Daniel moved with his family to Bellevue, WA where he also found musical success and enjoyment. While in Bellevue he received second place, and first place in the Washington State Solo Competition on Euphonium. He also had the opportunity to play as principal trombone in the Seattle Youth Symphony Orchestra. Daniel has performed in the 2010 Idaho All-State Band, the 2011 MENC All-Northwest band, and had the opportunity to play principal Euphonium in the 2012 NAfME All-National Band in Washington, D.C.


Daniel has also found success at Boise State. He has played as principal Euphonium in the Boise State University Symphonic Winds since 2012, played principal trombone in the BSU Symphony Orchestra for 7 semesters, and has played both Bass Trombone and Lead trombone in BSU Jazz Ensemble. He has also been a member of the BSU student brass quintet and BSU’s tuba-euphonium ensemble and trombone choir, which has been invited to perform at the 2017 International Trombone Festival. Daniel has also played in the Keith Stein Blue Thunder Marching Band for four years, three of those years as section leader, and performed many solos with the band. He won the Boise State Concerto-Aria Competition in 2014, and had the opportunity to perform the Horovitz Euphonium Concerto with the BSU Orchestra. Daniel won 2nd place in the 2015 Northwest Regional Tuba Euphonium Conference in the Euphonium Artist division.


Daniel has had the opportunity to study and learn from many great teachers, including Ryan Hartman, Danny Helseth, David Mathie, and Sarah Paradis.
